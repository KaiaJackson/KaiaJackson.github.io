Kaia Jackson

Actress

As an emerging actress from UC Riverside, I have experience both on film sets and on stage as an actress, director, and crew member. I’m a transfer student from Texas who attended school for two years in New York City where I discovered my passion for acting while studying forensic psychology. While in New York, I was trained in Adler and Stanislavski. I acted in the school’s production in 2023. I decided to change career paths and move across the country to pursue my dreams. Now in California, I have trained in Stanislavski further as well as Meisner. I have acted in short film projects from students as well as been an extra in a feature film. I act because it’s where I feel most alive—where passion, purpose, and storytelling meet. I’m drawn to raw, honest stories that explore mental health, social issues, and the complexity of being human. I’m especially passionate about destigmatizing mental health in creative spaces and making room for underrepresented voices in film. Whether it’s through performance or community outreach, I want to use storytelling as a tool for healing. With a background in psychology and a Southern upbringing, I bring depth, empathy, and a unique perspective to the characters I play. Beyond acting, I’m committed to advocacy, uplifting others, and breaking barriers in both creative and unconventional spaces. I see my journey as nonlinear, ever-evolving, and guided by curiosity, compassion, and the desire to make people feel seen and less alone. In the future, I hope to work on projects that blend psychological depth with social relevance—whether in independent films, theater, or television—and collaborate with creators who value authenticity, vulnerability, and bold storytelling. At the core, I believe storytelling has the power to shift hearts, challenge perspectives, and create deep human connection—and that’s what I strive for with every role.

Empathetic, Bold, & Soulful. Telling raw, emotionally honest stories that reflect the messy, beautiful truth of being human—and help people feel less alone in it. I’m most at home in stories that sit in the grey areas—the quiet moments, the heartbreak, the resilience. To me, acting isn’t just performance; it’s a form of human connection, a way of turning personal truth into something universal. Every role is an invitation to step inside someone else’s world and honor it with honesty and care.

I’m an actor who’s passionate about telling raw, emotionally grounded stories that explore the complexity of mental health, identity, and what it means to be human. I act because it’s where I feel most alive—where I can connect deeply with myself and others through honest storytelling. I’m drawn to dramatic roles that reflect the imperfect, often unspoken parts of life, especially narratives that create empathy and make people feel seen. My background in psychology and my Southern upbringing shape my perspective and fuel my desire to spark conversation around social issues, trauma, and healing. I bring empathy, curiosity, and boldness to the characters I play—and I believe in using my craft to help others feel less alone. Outside of acting, I’m passionate about racing and breaking barriers in advocacy, especially for women, mental health, and underrepresented voices. My journey is ever-evolving, and I’m committed to growing through every role I take on.
